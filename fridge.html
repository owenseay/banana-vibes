How far can one
banana go?

Rules

01 The banana is fixed.

Everything starts from and returns to the banana. It's the constant. If something can't be traced back to the banana, it's not part of the experiment.

02 AI does the building.

The human role is direction, curation, judgment, and synthesis. The experiment is about what AI can generate, compound, and sustain—not what a person can make with AI as a tool. The distinction matters because they represent very different ceilings.

03 The banana funds itself.

No external investment and no subsidy. The banana has to generate whatever resources it needs to keep expanding. If it can't, that's a valid answer to “How far can one banana go?” The constraint is the experiment.

04 Every output is input.

Each thing the banana becomes feeds what it can become next. An animation, a product, a piece of documentation, a revenue stream—nothing is terminal. The system either compounds or it dies.

05 Push into new territory.

The priority is discovery, not optimization. If a move or decision doesn't test whether the banana can go somewhere it hasn't yet been, that is considered maintenance, not experimentation.

06 Track everything.

A record of what happened is as valuable as what is produced. What worked, what didn't, what was unexpected. The experiment is only useful if it can be deciphered.

Log

March 15, 2026 Same banana, different trees

One banana starts at the center. After a couple seconds it spawns a smaller banana that drifts outward. That banana spawns its own, even smaller. Each generation gets smaller, drifts further, spawns slower. Faint golden lines connect each child back to its parent, tracing the lineage.

Every run produces a completely unique pattern, as every parameter that shapes the tree is driven by Math.random().

So every time the page loads or you hit the reset button, the root banana starts in the same center position, but the entire branching structure that grows from it will be different—different directions, timing, density, shape. You'll never see the same tree twice.

This is bananas.
